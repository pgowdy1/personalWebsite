Pacific Crest Trail

My 2,650 mile hike on the Pacific Crest Trail from Canada to Mexico.

  • Canadian Border

  • Washington

    Rain & Snow

    Starting at the border of Canada the 500+ mile Washington section of the PCT featured dense, lush forests and rocky, mystical mountain vistas. Navigating through the snow and rain for the first two weeks of the trail challenged me both physically and mentally as my body made the adjustment to the continual punishment of hiking 20+ miles every day.

  • Oregon

    Speed & Heat

    During the Oregon section of the trail my milage increased from 20-30 miles to 33+ miles per day. While the mid-summer heat was relentless in its intensity, I still crossed the volanic fields of the Three Sisters, summited Mt. Thielsen, and dangled my feet over the rocky cliffs of Crater Lake.

  • Northern California

    Hitting the Stride

    By Northern California I had adjusted to the rigors of thru-hiking and was able to enjoy the beauty of Castle Craggs, The Marble Wilderness, Lassen Volcanic National Park, and Mt. Shasta with a renewed sense of vitality. The spontaneous collapse of my hiking partner's lung saw a five day layover in Chico, CA where he received surgery and started working towards recovery.

  • Central California

    Majesty

    Considered by many to be the highlight of the entire PCT, the Sierra Nevada Mountains were undoubtedly some of the most gorgeous areas I have ever seen in print or person. This section was also the most strenuous as high-altitude, severe elevation change, and unpredictable weather all congregate to create potential disaster for the unprepared. The largest highlight was summiting Mt. Whitney, the highest mountain in the continental US, to see the sunrise on my 25th birthday--a momentous life event.

  • Southern California

    The Trek to the End

    Dry, hot, and not nearly as flat as one would think, the desert portion of the PCT tested my discipline and willpower to finish what I had started. Having already hiked 2000+ miles through some of the most beautiful areas of the U.S., the desert felt like a letdown as I slogged through the last 600 miles.
